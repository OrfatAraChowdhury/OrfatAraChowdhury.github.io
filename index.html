About Me

My name is Orfat Ara Chowdhury. I hold both a Bachelor's and Master's degree in English Language and Literature from the University of Chittagong. My academic journey has been not just about literary exploration, but also about developing strong analytical, communication, and leadership skills that I've applied beyond the classroom—especially in the realm of social change and community development.

With a strong commitment to equity and empowerment, I have been actively involved in volunteer work that addresses critical societal issues. As a teaching volunteer with Campaign RED, I work to debunk deeply ingrained taboos surrounding menstruation. My focus is on educating communities, especially young people, about menstrual health and hygiene, aiming to dismantle the harmful stigmas that continue to undermine the reproductive rights and social participation of women and girls.

At Shwapno Youth Development Organization, I not only volunteered in youth leadership programs but also led the Graphic Design Team, contributing to campaigns, workshops, and events that foster awareness and civic responsibility. My leadership and creativity merged here, allowing me to engage with young minds and collaborate with peers to amplify youth voices on national and international platforms.

In addition, I've served as a Teaching Assistant for the Youth Society for Research and Action (YSRA), where I supported foundational research education for students from diverse academic and cultural backgrounds. This role helped me deepen my understanding of academic research while also honing my mentoring and facilitation skills.

My participation in programs such as the Youth Leadership Bootcamp by BYLC, as well as workshops on public speaking, self-defense, and debating, has enriched my confidence and equipped me with the tools to advocate for myself and others effectively.

I am a strong believer in the power of knowledge-sharing and practical exposure. I aspire to work with mission-driven organizations where I can learn the intricacies of their operations, contribute meaningfully to their vision, and enhance their efficiency through innovative thinking and empathy-driven communication.

Beyond academics and advocacy, I am an explorer by nature. I find peace in traveling and connecting with the world around me—be it through nature, stories, or shared experiences. Each journey inspires me to remain grounded, compassionate, and curious.

Menstrual Hygiene Day 2018

Campaign Red celebrates Menstrual Hygiene Day and as a teaching volunteer of this organization I presented a poem about menstrual and also took part in the rally walking with the people and holding a placard that said 'Menstruation Matters'. Menstrual Hygiene Day is an annual awareness day on May 28 to highlight the importance of good menstrual hygiene management (MHM). It was initiated by the German-based NGO WASH United in 2014 and aims to benefit women and girls worldwide.

Joined as a volunteer in Campaign Red

I work on to debunk stigma and change long standing ideas and attitudes of society around menstruation that negatively have been affecting women's both reproductive and socio-economic rights. Campaign RED does not only engage youths to address the stigma around menstruation, it also advocates with schools and employers to bring awareness for creating opportunities for women to manage their menstruation in a dignified and safe way.

In the mid of Training

Creating a pool of trainers from Campaign RED's senior volunteers to be able to mentor our upcoming fellows.

Intervention at Goschi High School

As during puberty, girls begin their menstrual periods but they have a little knowledge about menstruation and thus have many misconceptions and taboo. My part was to educate these young people to have a clear picture about what to do and what not to do and also deal with awkward situations with dignity.
